The Problem

Hygiene Crisis at Street Food Carts

Millions of vendors struggle daily with a simple but serious problem — nowhere safe to put used plates.

🚨 The Core Issue

Street food chaat carts have minimal surface area, forcing vendors to stack used, wet, and dirty plates next to fresh food ingredients and water containers. The accumulation of used plates attracts flies, increasing contamination risks and compromising food hygiene.


Source: WHO Food Safety Report 2022, FAO Street Food Safety Guidelines, MDPI Home Science Journal

Who is affected?

  • 👥
    Consumers
    Face higher risk of foodborne illness from contaminated plates and flies.
  • 🛒
    Street Vendors
    Risk losing customers and income due to poor hygiene perception.
  • 🏛️
    Authorities & Municipalities
    Face increased monitoring burden and public health challenges.
Our Solution

TidyBite Edible Cups

Made from food-grade ragi and maida — strong, biodegradable, and delicious. No plates. No waste. No mess.

🌿 How It Works

TidyBite cups are baked using food-grade ragi and maida dough, shaped to hold both wet and dry chaat items. Once the food is eaten, the cup can be eaten too — or simply composted. Zero dirty plates, zero washing, zero contamination.


Core tech: Food-grade ragi & maida processing, dough molding, heat baking, and natural binding techniques.
